# South Suburb Expansion — Design Spec

Date: 2026-06-22
Status: Approved (auto mode — self-brainstormed)

## Goal

Grow the residential neighbourhood into a real district on the **opposite side of
town from the airport**, make the player's home a grand, heavily-detailed mansion
that is the **player spawn**, park the **drivable car** next to it, and connect the
whole district with a **road grid** plus a **driveway from the street to every
house** ("car trail to each home").

## Context (current state)

- World = object registry (`src/world/catalog/`) + map manifest (`src/world/map.ts`)
  + engine (`src/world/system/`). Authoring is data.
- Airport sits to the **NORTH** at world `(0, +260)`, reached by driving north on
  the `x=0` arterial.
- The suburb today is **west** at `(-205, 0)`: one E-W avenue + two N-S cross
  streets, a hero `playerHouse`, and **11** seeded placeholder `house` objects,
  generated by `suburbPlacements(ox, oz)` in `src/world/suburbMap.ts`, reached via a
  west connector expressway centred at `(-112, 0)`.
- Player + car spawn in the **city core** (`PLAYER_SPAWN {6, 3.8}`, `CAR_SPAWN
  {8, 1.5}`) in `src/world/map.ts`.
- `playerHouse` (`src/world/catalog/playerHouse.ts`) is a clean composite whose
  every child position derives from dimension constants at the top of `build()`
  (`W=12, D=11, FLOOR_H=3, FLOORS=2, GAR_W=6, GAR_D=7, DOOR_W=1.6`). Interior
  furniture is anchored to interior bounds (`inX/inZ`) or to a fixed `livCX`
  (`= -KITCHEN_BAND/2`, independent of `W`), so **growing `W`/`D` is safe** — the
  rooms get bigger and furniture stays wall-anchored / centred without overlap.

## Coordinate facts

- `+x` = east, `-x` = west, `+z` = north, `-z` = south. City centre at origin.
- City grid roads at `x,z ∈ {0, ±32, ±64}`; south city edge road at `z = -64`.
- `road` (ROAD_W = 6) and `airportRoad` run along `+x` at `rot 0`; `rot 90` makes a
  road run along `z`. `house`/`playerHouse` FRONT faces `+z`; `rot 180` → front
  faces world `-z` (south); `rot 0` → front faces north.
- Ground is `GROUND_SIZE = 820` (±410 from origin) — the south district (down to
  `z ≈ -270`) and the north airport both fit.

## Design

### 1. Relocate the district to the SOUTH

The airport is north, so the homes go **south** — the geometric opposite, "the other
side of town". Remove the west connector + west suburb; add:

- **South connector expressway**: an `airportRoad` along `z` (`rot 90`) at `x=0`,
  bridging the city south edge (`z≈-64`) to the district's north avenue (`z≈-180`),
  with lamps. Drive south on the `x=0` arterial straight into the neighbourhood,
  mirroring the northbound airport drive.
- `suburbPlacements(0, -210)` — district centred at `(0, -210)`.

### 2. Road grid connecting all houses

A connected grid (every lot fronts a paved road):

- **Two E-W avenues**: North Ave (`z ≈ -180`) and South Ave (`z ≈ -240`).
- **Three N-S cross streets** at `x ≈ {-72, 0, +72}` joining both avenues (the
  central one also meets the connector).
- Street lamps along the avenues.

### 3. ~4x the houses

From 11 → **~40 placeholder `house` objects** in four rows (north + south of each
avenue), each facing its avenue, plus a few cross-street corner lots. With the hero
house that is ~44 homes — roughly 4× the previous count. Houses are seeded variants
(existing `house` builder); placement derives from row z, an along-x pitch, and the
road bands (no house extent enters a road band; verified in comments like today's
file).

### 4. A driveway from the street to every house ("car trail")

New catalog object **`driveway`**: a flat paved strip (concrete slab with a subtle
pair of tyre tracks + edging), length derived from the lot's setback, width ~3 m.
One per house (incl. hero), oriented by `rot` to run from the fronting street up to
the house front. Visual only — no colliders (drivable/walkable).

### 5. Bigger hero house + 15+ exterior details

**Bigger** (`src/world/catalog/playerHouse.ts`): grow the footprint substantially —
main block `W 12→22`, `D 11→18`, `FLOOR_H 3→3.2`, a **2-car garage** `GAR_W 6→9`,
`GAR_D 7→8`. Keep `FLOORS=2` (avoids re-working the single-flight stair/hole logic)
and keep doors/windows/steps human-scale. This ~2.6× the floor area — clearly "way
bigger" — and is safe given the derivation notes above.

**15+ exterior details** (a new `makeFrontYard()` layer in the same builder, all
deterministic and derived from `W/D/DOOR_W`, entry path kept clear for spawn):

1. Walkable front **steps / stoop** up to the door (per-step colliders, same pattern
   as the interior stairs, rising the ~0.3 m to the floor slab).
2. A grand **entry walk** (paved slabs) from the stoop to the street.
3. **Flower beds** flanking the stoop (2), with coloured voxel blooms.
4. **Flower rows / borders** along the entry walk.
5. **Hedges / shrubs** flanking the façade (2–4).
6. **Lamp posts / path lanterns** at the walk entrance (2).
7. A **garden bench** near the porch.
8. **Potted plants** on the porch (2).
9. **Window flower boxes** under the front ground-floor windows (2).
10. **Path edging** stones / solar lights along the walk.
11. **Front-yard trees** (2).
12. Decorative **shrub balls / topiary** (2).
13. A **welcome mat** at the door.
14. A **house-number plaque / flag** by the door.
15. **Bird bath / garden ornament** centrepiece.

(Mailbox + perimeter fence + extra yard trees continue to come from the layout, so
the total comfortably exceeds 15 distinct detail elements.)

### 6. Spawn + drivable car at the hero house

- `PLAYER_SPAWN` → on the hero entry walk, just in front of the door (clear of fence,
  steps and furniture). The district becomes where the player wakes up.
- `CAR_SPAWN` + `CAR_SPAWN_YAW` → on the hero **driveway**, facing the street so you
  pull straight out. The static scenery `parkedCar` is removed from the hero lot (the
  real drivable car now occupies the driveway).

## Out of scope (YAGNI)

- No separate map/portal — the district stays part of the single city `MAP`, so
  moving `PLAYER_SPAWN` is all that "spawn at home" needs.
- No third storey on the hero house (keeps the stair/collider logic intact).
- No NPC crowds / traffic routing changes.

## Gate

`npx tsc --noEmit` clean **and** `npx vite build` succeeds. No dev server, no
screenshots, no tests (per `CLAUDE.md`). Then the user looks in-game.

## Assumptions & Decisions (auto-mode log)

- "Opposite side of town from the airport" → airport is NORTH (`z=+260`), so place
  the district to the **SOUTH** (`~z=-210`) and remove the old west suburb/connector
  — chose south because it is the geometric opposite of north.
- "~4x quantity" → 11 → **~40 placeholder houses** (+ hero ≈ 44) — chose ~40 to hit
  4× while every lot still fronts a road.
- "Roads connecting all the houses" → a **2-avenue + 3-cross-street grid** reusing
  the existing `road` kind — chose a grid (not one avenue) because 40 houses need
  frontage on multiple streets.
- "Roads from the road to the house / car trail" → a new **`driveway`** object
  (paved strip, length derived from setback), one per house — chose a dedicated
  reusable object over ad-hoc `pavement` so the "car trail" reads consistently.
- "Way bigger" hero house → **~2.6× footprint** (W 12→22, D 11→18, 2-car garage),
  `FLOORS` kept at 2 — chose footprint growth over adding a floor to avoid reworking
  the stair/stairwell-hole logic; doors/steps stay human-scale.
- "15 details" → a **`makeFrontYard()`** detail layer in the builder (door-anchored
  details: steps, walk, flowers, planters, hedges, lamps, bench, pots, flower boxes,
  ornaments) + layout yard props — chose to bind details to the house so they scale
  with it and satisfy the "derive from dimensions" rule.
- "Spawn of the player" → move `PLAYER_SPAWN` to the hero entry walk; keep one map
  (no portal) — simplest mechanism that satisfies "spawn at home".
- "Car near it" → move the **drivable** `CAR_SPAWN` to the hero driveway and remove
  the static scenery `parkedCar` from the hero lot — avoids two cars overlapping.
- Worktree → **work on `master`** (user's standing rule: no worktrees/branches) — log
  per autonomous-builder; isolation rule overridden by explicit user instruction.
- Build process → enlarge-house and driveway-object are independent and self-
  contained → done as **parallel subagents**; the suburb-grid + map/spawn/car
  integration (which depends on both) is done **directly** for coherent overlap math.
